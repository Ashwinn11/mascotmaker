## 1. Latent Space and the "Randomness" of AI

To understand why consistency is hard, we have to look at how AI (like Stable Diffusion or DALL-E) works. 

### Images from Noise
When you prompt a general-purpose AI, it starts with a canvas of random static (noise). It then "denoises" that static to find the image that matches your prompt. The problem? Every tiny variation in that noise seed leads to a completely different "path" for the character. 

This is why, in a standard AI model, even if you keep the same prompt (e.g., "a 3D dragon barista"), the AI will give you 10 different-looking dragons. It has no "memory" of what that specific dragon looked like.

---

## 2. Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRAs) and the "Heavy Lifting"

The most common "pro" solution has been **LoRAs (Low-Rank Adaptations)**. 

### Training is Slow and Expensive
To get a consistent character, you usually have to "train" a custom model on 20+ photos of that specific character from different angles. 
- **The Problem:** Startup founders don't have time to train a custom model.
- **The Friction:** If you want to change your mascot's style (e.g., from [Pixar 3D](/mascot-maker/style/3d-pixar) to [Claymation](/mascot-maker/style/claymation)), you have to start the training over from scratch.

### The Middle Ground
Some tools use "Face Swap" technology (like ReActor or InsightFace), but these only handle the face. They fail at preserving the materials, textures, and gear (like a specific scarf or a hat) that make a mascot feel like a cohesive brand asset.
